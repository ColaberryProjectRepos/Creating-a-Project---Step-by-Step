Here is a Guide:


KPI - You should be using Industry KPIs.  The more important the KPI, the more you will get the buy in from the business that this is indeed an issue.

Good or Bad - Is this Insight Positive or Negative for the company.

Responsible Dim - Time will always be one of your Dims because you will always be comparing some time period to another.  If your insight also includes slicing the data by another dimension, it will also be mentioned here.  

What is wrong - This will always be related to KPI.  Did it increase, decrease, double, triple?

When the problem occurred -  You will always need to reference the time period in which this wrong doing occurred.  Try to use enough time period where a trend can be established.  You will need to specify the actual time periods.  Don't say things like "6 months ago" because if someone goes through this project a year from now, they will not know which dates you are talking about.

The Extent of the Problem - You will always have 2 periods of Data.  The problem period which is the time and/or filters which are on during the problem. You will also have period of normality which are the time/filters that are on when there is no problem. 

These 2 periods should not consist of the same data.  For example, if I am comparing Open Rate for Nov 2019 and comparing it to Nov 2018.  Those are two separate sets of data. 

Another example ...if I was comparing Gross Profit Margin of Gloves YTD compared to all other Clothing items YTD.  Gloves sales would be included in the 1st set, but not the 2nd set.  They are using the same time periods, but still separate sets of data.

Once you know the 2 periods, you will calculate the KPI amount in both periods.  Then you will do a % increase calculation on both numbers to calculate the significance of the problem. Plug your numbers into the Percentage Increase Calculator.

Initial Amount: KPI of period before the problem

Final Amount: KPI of period during problem period
