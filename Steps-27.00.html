All Step By Step Instructions should START  with screenshots of what the user can expect to build by following your instructions

All Step By STep Instructions should END with screenshots of what the user can expect to have built by following your instructions

Just as you will view several projects on Colaberry Network, several people will view your project.  Do not make any assumptions that the user knows anything.  Your goal should be to create Step by Step instructions as if the user has no knowledge of what you are doing.  That means if there was an article or video that helped you understand a concept better, then add it to your documentation.  Take all the chances you can to teach.  Use lots of icons and screenshots and gifs.  The more pictures the better.

Step by Step instructions will start immediately after the messaging documentation.  Your goal is to get the user to complete all of the steps while successfully recreating your project.  The better you document it the easier it will be for them to go through and follow each step.

GIFs - GIFs are going to be very important here because step by step instructions can get very tedious so instead of taking 100 screenshots, you will instead describe the steps in less than 30 second video clips called gifs.  
Although there is an earlier step "Adding Project Instrutions" that specifies how to add a question, this is specific to step by step instructions.


1.  Step Name - This is a very short phrase to describe the step.

2. Data File - This is where you will upload all of the files needed for this step.  Upload them in zip files ONLY.

3. Detailed Instructions - This is where you will add the detailed instructions for each step.  Use Gifs to condense instructions.  We don't want it to

4. Insight - These are loaded at the end of the question.  All Step by Step Instructions, you will provide insight for.  That means a video or article that further helps a struggling user.  This is just to give them a resource that will dive more into the topic of the step.  Maybe it's a download for the tool you are using.

Note: You should already have the majority of the information you need from Stress Test 2 & 3.  You will need to add insight to every step and mask your links (Don't show the long link but instead have small text describing the link, but opens in a another tab when clicked on) 


Here are some more rules to follow...
  • Be sure to upload any files needed in the Data File section.  They should be uploaded as a zip file ONLY.  If you have multiple files be sure to label them accordingly and put them all in the zip file before uploading
  • Number your steps within a Question so it is clear what to do.
  • Use GIFs as much as possible and Keep your GIF's under 4 mb
  • Keep your Steps subject based.  If they don't relate to the step name, create another step.  Do not create really long steps.  It tires the user out.  It's better for them to have to click to move to the next step then keep scrolling
  • We are tracking all of the clicks so we know where people struggle throughout your project.  We will show those stats later.

Use the power of numbers to learn from others.  Go view a few projects and take what you like about each one and create your own process.  FYI - The most clicked on projects in Colaberry Network show up 1st.



Instructions:

  1. Assign this task to yourself.
  2. Once you have read through this task and you fully understand it, write a small paragraph with screenshots and notes of what you learned.  Then add it as a comment on this task.
  3. Everyday you work on this task, you will add a minimal of a comment and a screenshot.  Gifs are good as well.
  4. Note: You as well as other people will be reading through your project notes.  Make sure you talk about your thoughts at the moment so you and others can relive the experience.
  5. Upon completion, close the task and navigate back to your Colaberry Project Dashboard.
